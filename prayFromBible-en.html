Our Father in heaven, hallowed be Your name — Interpretation

“Our Father in heaven” — we address God as our loving Father, acknowledging His authority, care, and holiness.

“Hallowed be Your name” — a request that God’s name be glorified in our lives and in this world. May people come to know Him and honor Him as Holy.

The prayer begins with worship and recognition of God’s greatness.

Your Kingdom come; Your will be done on earth as it is in heaven — Interpretation

“Your Kingdom come” — a prayer that God’s Kingdom would come into the hearts of people, into the world, and into our lives. It is a longing for justice, peace, and God’s love among us.

“Your will be done” — acknowledging God’s authority and trusting His will. It’s a readiness to follow God’s commandments and accept His guidance in our lives.

“On earth as it is in heaven” — a desire for the earth to reflect the heavenly order where only God’s will is fulfilled. We pray for God’s truth and love to reign on earth.

Give us this day our daily bread — Interpretation

“Give us this day our daily bread” — a prayer asking God to provide for our daily needs: food, health, protection, wisdom. It is an expression of trust in God every day.

Bread is a symbol of both material blessings and spiritual nourishment for the soul.

And forgive us our debts, as we also have forgiven our debtors — Interpretation

“And forgive us our debts” — a prayer asking God to forgive our sins and mistakes.

“As we also have forgiven our debtors” — a reminder that we must forgive others in order to receive forgiveness ourselves. It teaches mercy, forgiveness, and freedom from resentment.

And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from the evil one. For Yours is the Kingdom and the power and the glory forever. Amen — Interpretation

“And lead us not into temptation” — a prayer asking God to protect us from evil and help us stand firm in trials.

“But deliver us from the evil one” — a plea for protection from Satan and all evil, that God’s power would be our shield.

“For Yours is the Kingdom and the power and the glory forever” — a declaration of God’s authority, power, and eternal glory. The prayer ends with words of praise.
